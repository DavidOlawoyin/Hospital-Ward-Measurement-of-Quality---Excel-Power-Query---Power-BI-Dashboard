# 🏥 Objective
This project analyses key hospital quality indicators to monitor compliance with clinical standards and support patient safety improvements. It focuses on ward-level performance across critical areas including patient falls, hand hygiene, nutrition and hydration, and pressure ulcer prevention.

## 🚨 Problem Statement

Healthcare organisations are required to continuously monitor essential quality indicators to ensure patient safety and maintain regulatory compliance. However, these metrics are often stored in separate spreadsheets, making it difficult to gain a unified view of performance or track trends over time.

This lack of integrated visibility limits the ability of healthcare teams to identify performance gaps, detect non-compliance early, and take proactive corrective actions. As a result, patient safety risks may increase, and opportunities for quality improvement may be missed.

This project addresses these challenges by consolidating multiple datasets into a single analytical solution, enabling effective monitoring of compliance rates against a defined 90% standard benchmark and supporting data-driven decision-making.

## 📂 Dataset Overview

The project uses multiple datasets representing different hospital quality indicators:

* **Falls & Injury Management** – Tracks patient fall incidents and compliance levels
* **Hand Hygiene Compliance** – Measures adherence to hygiene protocols
* **Nutrition & Hydration** – Monitors patient care related to nutrition standards
* **Pressure Ulcer Prevention** – Evaluates compliance in preventing pressure ulcers

Each dataset includes:

* Month and Year
* Number of compliant cases
* Number of observations
* Calculated compliance percentage
* Standard benchmark (90%)

## ⚙️ Data Processing & Workflow

* Cleaned and structured raw Excel data using **Excel (Power Query)**
* Standardised columns across datasets for consistency
* Calculated compliance rates and created performance indicators
* Loaded cleaned data into **Power BI** for data visualization

* Most indicators maintain high compliance levels close to the 90% benchmark
* Occasional drops in compliance highlight potential risk periods requiring attention
* Hand hygiene shows noticeable variability compared to other indicators
* Nutrition, hydration, and pressure ulcer prevention demonstrate more stable performance
* Fall and injury management maintains consistently high compliance with minor fluctuations
